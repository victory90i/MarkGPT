# 🔑 Contribution Code of Conduct

All contributors to the MarkGPT LLM Curriculum are expected to adhere to this Code of Conduct. We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and professional environment for everyone.

---

## Our Values

### 🤝 **Respect**
Treat all contributors, learners, and community members with dignity and respect, regardless of their background, experience level, or identity.

### 📚 **Inclusion**
Welcome people from all backgrounds, cultures, and experience levels. Make space for new voices and perspectives.

### 🌱 **Growth**
Support learning and growth. Remember that everyone started as a beginner. Patience and mentorship are valued.

### 🎯 **Integrity**
Maintain honesty and transparency in all interactions. Give and receive feedback constructively.

### 🌍 **Cultural Sensitivity**
Respect cultural knowledge and intellectual property, especially regarding African languages and heritage. Attribute sources appropriately.

---

## Expected Behavior

✅ **Do:**

- Be respectful and constructive in all communication
- Welcome feedback and help others improve
- Use inclusive language (avoid jargon when possible)
- Credit others' ideas and work
- Assume good intent, but address impact
- Report concerns to maintainers
- Support other contributors
- Celebrate others' contributions
- Listen actively
- Ask for help when needed

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

❌ **Don't:**

- Use derogatory language or personal attacks
- Dismiss others' ideas based on their experience level
- Make assumptions about people's backgrounds or abilities
- Plagiarize or misrepresent others' work
- Engage in harassment or bullying (including off-platform)
- Make unwanted sexual advances
- Violate others' intellectual property
- Share others' private information without consent
- Spam or self-promote excessively
- Discriminate based on race, gender, religion, sexual orientation, disability, or any protected characteristic

---

## Specific to MarkGPT Community

### Regarding Banso Language & African Heritage

- ✅ Respectfully engage with Banso language and culture
- ✅ Cite sources appropriately
- ✅ Defer to native speakers on linguistic accuracy
- ✅ Acknowledge the cultural significance of the project
- ✅ Collaborate with community members
- ❌ Don't claim credit for cultural knowledge
- ❌ Don't appropriate or misrepresent language/culture
- ❌ Don't make assumptions about the language or people

### Data Privacy & Ethics

- ✅ Protect contributors' private information
- ✅ Use data ethically and transparently
- ✅ Follow all relevant laws (GDPR, etc.)
- ❌ Don't share personal data without consent
- ❌ Don't use data for purposes not disclosed
- ❌ Don't commit sensitive information (credentials, API keys)

---

## Conflict Resolution

### If You Witness a Violation

1. **If safe, address it directly** — A brief message pointing out the concern
2. **Contact maintainers privately** — Email iwstechnical@gmail.com with details
3. **Documentation** — Keep records of the incident

### If You're Called Out

- **Listen first** — Try to understand the concern
- **Don't argue defensively** — Ask clarifying questions
- **Reflect** — Consider the impact, not just intent
- **Apologize sincerely** — If you made a mistake
- **Change behavior** — Follow through on your commitment
- **Move forward** — Past mistakes don't define future participation

### Investigation Process

The maintainers will:
1. Acknowledge receipt of report
2. Investigate impartially
3. Speak with all parties (when safe)
4. Determine violations
5. Take appropriate action
6. Follow up with reporter

---

## Enforcement

### Possible Actions (escalating)

1. **Warning** — Clarification of expectations
2. **Temporary Mute** — Temporary restriction from participation
3. **Suspension** — Removal from project for a period
4. **Permanent Ban** — Removal due to serious or repeated violations

### Right of Appeal

Anyone who believes they've been unjustly treated can appeal to the project governance within 30 days of action.

## Examples

### ✅ Good Community Interaction

**Scenario:** You see a PR with a typo in the commit message

```
❌ Bad: "Wow, learn to spell before contributing 🙄"

✅ Good: "Hey! Small typo in the commit message: 'learhing' → 'learning'. 
Would you like to fix it?"
```

**Scenario:** Someone makes a technical suggestion you disagree with

```
❌ Bad: "That's a stupid idea. It won't work."

✅ Good: "Interesting idea! I'm concerned about [specific issue]. 
Have you considered [alternative approach]? Let me know what you think."
```

**Scenario:** Someone submits their first PR with imperfect code

```
❌ Bad: "This code is terrible. Do you even know Python?"

✅ Good: "Welcome! Your idea is great. Just a small suggestion here 
[specific feedback]. Here's a resource that might help: [link]. 
Thanks for contributing!"
```

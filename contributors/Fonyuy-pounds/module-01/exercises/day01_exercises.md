## Exercise E01.1 — The AI Family Tree (Conceptual, Paper + Pen)

**Estimated time:** 20 minutes  
**Materials:** Paper, pen, this lesson

**Instructions:**

Without looking anything up, draw the lineage of AI from 1950 to today. Your diagram should show:

- Major eras (symbolic AI, connectionism, deep learning, LLMs)
- Key milestones and their approximate dates (even rough decades are fine)
- The transitions: what broke each era? What sparked the next?
- Where MarkGPT sits in this timeline

There is no "correct" diagram. The goal is to build a mental map in your own hand. When you are done, compare it to the narrative in Lesson 1.1 and annotate any gaps or surprises.

**Reflection question (write 2–3 sentences):** What surprised you most about this history? What would you have predicted differently if you were a researcher in 1956?

## Exercise E01.2 — Hands-On with a Language Model

**Estimated time:** 30 minutes  
**Materials:** A web browser, access to any LLM (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or GPT-2 on Hugging Face Spaces)

**Part A — Baseline English Prompts**

Try these five prompts and record the outputs:
1. `"In the beginning, God created the heavens and"`
2. `"The covenant between God and Abraham was a promise that"`
3. `"Complete this sentence: The shepherd leaves the ninety-nine to"`
4. `"Write a one-sentence proverb about patience."`
5. `"What does the word 'covenant' mean in the context of the Old Testament?"`

**Part B — Language Variation**

Now try the same prompts (or equivalents) in a language other than English that you know, or try:
- Pidgin English (e.g., *"Na so God make heaven and earth begin"*)
- Formal archaic English (thee/thou forms)
- A local dialect you are familiar with

**Part C — Observation Journal**

Write at least one paragraph answering each of these questions:
- How does the model's output change with language or register?
- Where does it feel convincing? Where does it break down?
- What does this tell you about what the model has and hasn't been trained on?

## Exercise E01.3 — The Reflection Journal: Day 1 Entry

**Estimated time:** 10 minutes  
**Materials:** The file `modules/module-01/journal/day01_journal.md` (or a notebook)

Answer these three questions in full sentences — not bullet points:

1. "After reading Lesson 1.1 and trying E01.2, what is my current mental model of what a language model *is*? Not the technical details — what does it *do* and *why does it do it*?"

2. "What does training a model on Banso text mean culturally? Why might this matter beyond the technical achievement?"

3. "What specific thing do I want to be able to do by the end of Day 60 that I cannot do today?"

Keep your Day 1 journal entry. On Day 60, you will re-read it, and the distance between those two entries will be one of the most satisfying things you have ever read about your own learning.

## Bonus Challenge (Optional, for the Curious)

Find and read the first two pages of Turing's 1950 paper "Computing Machinery and Intelligence." It is freely available online and written in elegant English that requires no technical background. After reading:

Write a paragraph: *If Turing could run MarkGPT today, what would he think? Would MarkGPT pass the Imitation Game?*

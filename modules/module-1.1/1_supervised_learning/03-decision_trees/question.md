# Decision Trees - Deep Dive Questions

## Question 1: Tree Building Algorithm
Explain the recursive splitting process in decision tree construction. What is entropy/gini impurity and how do they guide the splitting process? What's the stopping criterion?

## Question 2: Overfitting in Trees
Why are decision trees prone to overfitting? How does max_depth, min_samples_split, and min_samples_leaf help prevent overfitting? What's the bias-variance trade-off?

## Question 3: Feature Importance
How are feature importances calculated in decision trees? What does an importance score of 0.8 mean? Can feature importance be misleading in certain scenarios?

## Question 4: Information Gain vs. Gini
Compare information gain and Gini impurity as splitting criteria. Are they equivalent? What are the computational and practical differences?

## Question 5: Handling Non-linear Relationships
Why are decision trees naturally suited to capture non-linear relationships compared to linear models? Can they capture all types of non-linearities?

## Question 6: Interpretability and Business Applications
How does interpretability of decision trees compare to other algorithms? How would you extract business rules from a trained tree? What are the limitations?

## Question 7: Multi-class and Regression
How do decision trees handle multi-class classification? How does regression with decision trees differ from classification? What's the prediction mechanism for continuous targets?

## Question 8: Missing Values and Categorical Features
How do decision trees handle missing values? How are categorical features typically encoded or handled during splitting? What are better alternatives?

## Question 9: Scalability and Complexity
What is the time and space complexity of decision tree training? How do trees scale with data size and dimensionality? Are there scenarios where trees become impractical?

## Question 10: Trees vs. Ensemble Methods
Why are individual decision trees often weak learners? How do ensemble methods (Random Forests, XGBoost) improve upon single decision trees? What's the conceptual foundation of these improvements?

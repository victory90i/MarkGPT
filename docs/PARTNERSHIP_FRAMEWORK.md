# Banso Language Partnership Framework

## Mission & Values

### Our Commitment to Language Preservation

MarkGPT with Banso language support aims to:

1. **Preserve** endangered languages through technology
2. **Empower** linguistic communities through access
3. **Respect** intellectual property and cultural heritage
4. **Distribute** benefits equitably within communities
5. **Collaborate** with speakers as partners, not subjects

---

## Partnership Principles

### Principle 1: Community Ownership
- Community has final say on how language is used
- Revenue from Banso-specific features goes back to community
- Community advisory board makes strategic decisions

### Principle 2: Transparent Data Use
- All data sourcing documented publicly
- Consent forms in local language
- Annual report on data usage

### Principle 3: Knowledge Sharing
- Free model access for community members
- Training workshops for local developers
- Technology transfer to community institutions

### Principle 4: Benefit Sharing
```
Revenue Model (if applicable):
├─ 50% Community Development Fund
│  ├─ Language education programs
│  ├─ Digital infrastructure
│  ├─ Youth training initiatives
│  └─ Cultural preservation projects
├─ 30% Ongoing Research & Development
├─ 15% Operations & Maintenance
└─ 5% Misc. (contingency, audits)
```

### Principle 5: Sovereignty & Control
- Community can request data removal
- Community can limit model distribution
- Access can be restricted to community members if desired


### 4. Governance
**Community Advisory Board**:
- 3-5 community representatives
- Quarterly meetings
- Vote on major decisions:
  - [ ] Distribution of models
  - [ ] Revenue allocation
  - [ ] Data additions/removals
  - [ ] New features

**MarkGPT Responsibilities**:
- Provide data governance tools
- Secure data infrastructure
- Regular audits and transparency reports
- Technical support for community developers

### 5. Benefit Distribution

| Year | Community Fund | Research | Operations |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $0 (pilot) | 100% | - |
| 2+ | If revenue | 50% | 30% | 15% | 5% |

**Community Fund Allocation** (example):
- 40%: Education & training programs
- 30%: Infrastructure & tools

### 6. Duration & Renewal
- **Initial**: 3-year commitment
- **Renewal**: Mutual agreement, terms renegotiable
- **Exit**: 90-day notice, data gracefully retired

## Historical Context: Why This Matters

### Banso Language Facts
- **Speakers**: ~500,000 people (declining)
- **Region**: West African (Nigeria, Cameroon)
- **Status**: Severely endangered (UNESCO)
- **Digital Presence**: Minimal (no spell-check, no LLM resources)
- **Educational**: Often not taught formally

### Technology Impact
- Most NLP tools focused on high-resource languages (English, Mandarin, Spanish)
- Modern NLP requires large datasets (Banso has few public ones)
- LLMs can help preserve and revitalize endangered languages

### MarkGPT Contribution
- Creates bilingual EN-Banso LLM
- Generates synthetic Banso training data
- Enables text generation in Banso
- Foundation for Banso NLP research

## Accountability & Transparency

### Data Governance
- **Annual Audit**: Independent review of data usage
- **Consent Log**: Track who provided what data, for what purpose
- **Privacy Safeguards**: Encryption, access controls, retention limits
- **Community Access**: Dashboard showing data usage in real-time

### Financial Transparency
- Quarterly financial reports
- Community review of budget allocation
- Third-party audit confirmation
- Open-source accounting data (anonymized)

### Impact Measurement
**Metrics We Track**:
- Students trained per year
- Jobs created
- Publications/research
- Community software created
- Languages reached (if expanded)

**Annual Report** includes:
- Data usage statistics
- Financial summary
- Impact stories
- Recommendations for year ahead
